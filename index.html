Etiam Capillus Unus Habet Umbram Suam
Technical writing and critical reflection on technology
aug 1986 – oct 2022

Alongside my professional career, I have consistently cultivated a keen interest in technical writing, scientific communication, and the philosophy of knowledge and technological responsibility. During the 1990s, I contributed to a range of specialist publications, including CQ Elettronica, Linux Journal, Elettronica Flash, Radio Rivista, RadioKit Elettronica, Ubuntu Magazine, Windows & .Net Magazine, Hackin9, and Red Hat Magazine, writing on subjects such as the Linux operating system, software-defined radio (SDR), cybersecurity, open source culture, and IT governance.
In 1999, I was among the first to introduce Linux to the Italian amateur radio community, advocating the use of free software in both scientific and educational settings.
Today, I contribute to the Stultifera Navis project, where I explore the interplay between systems thinking, technical mediation, and institutional memory, with particular attention to the materiality of information and emerging digital epistemologies.

Virtute Siderum Tenus
Italian Air Force
oct 1991 – jun 1996

I joined the Italian Air Force in October 1991 and attended the Air Force War College (Scuola di Guerra Aerea) in Florence, before specialising in telecommunications at the Technical Training Unit (Reparto Tecnico Addestrativo) of the 14th Wing (14° Stormo) in Pratica di Mare. I was subsequently assigned to the 41st Anti-Submarine Warfare Wing (41° Stormo Antisom) in Sigonella, where I served as Officer in Charge of the Operational Services Unit. Upon promotion to the rank of Lieutenant (Tenente), I assumed command of a NATO Communications Centre (ACCAM). For two consecutive years, I also held the additional role of Director of the Officers’ Club and the officers’ and NCO messes. During this period, I worked with the Information and Operational Situation Service (S.I.O.S.), the 34th RADAR Group (34° Gruppo RADAR), and the 11th Aircraft Maintenance Unit (11° Reparto Manutenzione Velivoli) as a telecommunications and radio signal analysis specialist. I was honourably discharged in May 1995 and received a formal commendation for exemplary conduct and outstanding service. Until June 1996, I continued to support operations as an external consultant, ensuring continuity and providing training on custom software solutions for document management and information analysis that I had developed.
