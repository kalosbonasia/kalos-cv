Articoli

I am tired of having to polish every word, every gesture, every detail—as if appearance mattered more than substance. It is exhausting to always appear up to standard, just to avoid being discarded like that slightly dull apple no one chooses at the supermarket. The truth is, many people would not even recognise that apple for what it is. They have never stood beneath an apple tree, a pear tree, a cherry tree. They do not know the scent of real earth, nor the difference between what grows and what simply glitters yet is rotten inside.

I write because I prefer substance to decorum, observation to self-satisfaction. I have worked in systems, with systems, sometimes against them. An Air Force officer in my youth, then a project manager, then an independent consultant: I have learnt to navigate complexity without needing to sell it.

On Stultifera Navis, I seek a space where thought can appear naked, without scenery. I have no truths to defend, nor models to promote. Only the urge to set down, in order, what still seems to me worth saying.
